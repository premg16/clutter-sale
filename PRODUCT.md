# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences share this homepage:
- **Donors/sellers**: people with unused stuff at home (moving out, spring cleaning, inherited items, life transitions) who haven't yet decided to sell/donate. They arrive undecided, not pre-sold — the page has to make the emotional case before asking for a download.
- **Nonprofits**: organizations that could partner with or be listed on the platform, evaluating whether this is a credible, well-run operation worth their trust.

## Product Purpose

Clutter Sale is a marketplace where people sell their unused items and 100% of proceeds go to a nonprofit the seller chooses. The homepage's job is emotional buy-in first: convince a visitor that decluttering-for-charity is a compelling, low-friction, trustworthy act — before asking them to download the app. Conversion (app download) matters but should never feel rushed ahead of the emotional case.

## Brand Personality

Calm, playful, emotionally persuasive. Not cold or corporate (the explicit failure mode of the original homepage this replaced), but not chaotic either — warmth and a little delight, with real emotional weight behind the cause itself. Second-person-adjacent, direct, short copy. Illustrated/collage visual language (hand-drawn stickers, bold color-blocked sections, oversized display type) rather than muted SaaS minimalism.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / fintech landing page**: the original starting point for this redesign — muted terracotta/sage palette, serif elegance, subtle fade-up animations, clean rounded cards. Reads as competent but interchangeable with any B2B product; explicitly being designed away from.
- **Generic charity-org solemnity**: heavy guilt-driven framing, somber stock photography, obligation-based messaging. The brand wants delight and agency ("send it forward"), not guilt.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the mechanism, not just the mission.** Trust comes from visible proof (real photos, dollar amounts, nonprofit names, seller quotes), not abstract claims about "impact."
2. **Earn the "wow" through illustration and motion, not gradients.** Hand-drawn stickers, bold color-blocked sections, and oversized type do the emotional work — not gradient text or glassmorphism.
3. **Second-person, concrete copy over aphorism.** Short sentences describing what literally happens ("sell it, send it forward") rather than inspirational abstraction.
4. **Every section earns its place in a persuasion arc.** Hook → credibility (stats) → problem → solution (how it works) → self-identification (who it's for) → proof (causes + testimonials) → objection handling (FAQ) → close (CTA). Not decoration for its own sake.
5. **Delight without undermining trust.** Playful stickers and bold type coexist with real photos, real numbers, and a visible security/trust signal — the platform must feel credible to nonprofits, not just fun to donors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast throughout. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already implemented via Lenis smooth-scroll fallback and Framer Motion `useReducedMotion` guards on parallax effects).
